Control Flow and Loops

Control Flow and Loops are most basic and important foundations for any programming language. Any simple meaningful program must at least has control flow, loops or both. Let's consider this simple analogy before explaining Control Flow and Loops. Imagine a school teacher who gaves his students a paper exam with one true-false question. The teacher grabs a pile of exams on his right and picks the exam for some student for grading, he looks at the answer and gives a point if the answer is true or no point if the answer is false. The next step is he puts the paper to hid left side. Then he grabs the next paper from the pile on his right. Teacher A repeats these steps until there are no papers to grade. If you think about it for a moment you'll that the process of grading the exam is very mechanical and systematic and can be programmed. There are collection of things that we want to process until some kind of condition is met in order to terminate. If the answer is correct then a point is given, else do nothing. IF Conditions is one of most common used control flows and it tells the computer to either process or skip a computation based on a condition. In our example, the condition is either the student has provided the correct answer or not. Since there are a collection of things to process (exam answers), we need a loop to traverse the collection. There are two ways of achieving this and here is a psuedo-code:
